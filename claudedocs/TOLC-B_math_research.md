### 6 Thematic Nuclei (Nuclei Tematici)

#### NUCLEO 1: Numeri (Numbers)
- Operations: addition, multiplication on integers, fractions, decimals
- Ordering and ordering properties
- Subtraction and division
- Concept of rational number
- Number line representations; real numbers
- Integer division with remainder
- GCD (MCD) and LCM (mcm) of two or more positive integers
- Integer powers and power properties
- Positive integer root of a positive number
- Powers with rational exponents of a positive number
- Estimation and approximation
- Percentages
- Calculation and transformation of simple expressions

#### NUCLEO 2: Algebra
- Literal expressions: manipulation and evaluation
- Concept of solution and "solution set" for equations, inequalities, systems
- First and second degree algebraic equations and inequalities (or reducible to them)
- Linear systems

#### NUCLEO 3: Geometria (Geometry)
- Plane and space figures: segments, lines, planes, angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, circles, parallelepipeds, prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, spheres -- properties and representations
- Pythagorean theorem
- Properties of similar triangles
- Elementary geometric transformations (symmetries, rotations, translations, similarities) and their effects
- Sine, cosine, tangent of an angle (as ratios of sides in a right triangle)
- Perimeter and area of main plane figures; volume of elementary solids; area/volume by sum/difference
- Cartesian coordinates in the plane; describing subsets using coordinates
- Slope of a segment and equation of a line
- Equations of parallel and perpendicular lines
- Intersection of lines and representation of solutions of a first-degree system
- Distance between two points; equation of a circle with given center and radius

#### NUCLEO 4: Funzioni e Grafici (Functions and Graphs)
- Function language, notation, graphs
- Composition of functions
- Existence and uniqueness of solutions of f(x) = a; invertible functions and inverse function
- Relative maxima/minima; monotonicity intervals
- Characteristic properties, graphs, and behavior of:
  - Power functions and root functions
  - First and second degree polynomial functions
  - Functions of type x -> 1/(ax+b) with given constants a, b
  - Absolute value function
  - Exponential functions and logarithmic functions in various bases
  - Sine and cosine functions
- Definition of logarithm and elementary algebraic properties of exponential/logarithm based on power properties
- Equations and inequalities expressed via functions, e.g. f(x) = g(x), f(x) > a

#### NUCLEO 5: Combinatoria e Probabilita (Combinatorics and Probability)
- Representation and counting of finite sets
- Disjoint events; independent events
- Probability of union of disjoint events; probability of intersection of independent events
- Description of events in paradigmatic situations: coin toss, dice roll, drawing from an urn
- Tree diagrams
- Conditional probability

#### NUCLEO 6: Medie e Variabilita (Statistics: Means and Variability)
- Qualitative and quantitative variables (discrete and continuous)
- Absolute and relative frequency
- Distribution representations: tables, bar graphs, pie charts, histograms
- Mean and median

### 3 Cross-Cutting Nuclei (Nuclei Trasversali)

#### TRASVERSALE 1: Comprendere e Rappresentare (Understand and Represent)
- Understand texts using multiple languages and representations simultaneously
- Use different representations of the same mathematical object depending on situation/goals
- Understand and use elementary set notation: element, belongs to, subset, union, intersection

#### TRASVERSALE 2: Argomentare (Reasoning/Arguing)
- Given a situation and premises, determine if a statement is true or false
- Negate a given statement
- Understand and use: "for each", "all", "none", "some", "at least one", "if...then...", necessary condition, sufficient condition, necessary and sufficient condition

#### TRASVERSALE 3: Modellizzare, Risolvere Problemi (Modeling, Problem Solving)
- Formulate a situation or problem in mathematical terms
- Solve a problem by adopting simple strategies, combining different knowledge and skills, making logical deductions and simple calculations

### SET A: Official CISIA Example Test (20 questions -- TOLC-S/TOLC-B shared math section)

**Q1** [Percentages/Time] Uno studente ha a disposizione 2 ore per svolgere una prova d'esame. Quando e trascorso il 79% del tempo, quanto manca al termine della prova?
- A: 25 min 24 sec | B: 25 min 12 sec | C: 25 min 36 sec | D: 25 min 48 sec | E: 25 min 20 sec
- **Correct: B** (21% of 120 min = 25.2 min = 25 min 12 sec)
- Topic: Numeri/Percentuali | Difficulty: EASY | Competency: calculation, percentage conversion

**Q2** [Logarithms] Uno solo dei numeri seguenti e compreso tra 2 e 3. Quale?
- A: log_2(30) | B: log_3(30) | C: log_5(30) | D: log_6(30) | E: log_10(30)
- **Correct: B** (3^2=9, 3^3=27 so log_3(30) is between 2 and 3... actually log_3(27)=3 and log_3(30)>3. Let me reconsider: log_5(25)=2, log_5(125)=3, so log_5(30) is between 2 and 3.)
- **Correct: C** (log_5(25)=2, log_5(125)=3, 25<30<125)
- Topic: Funzioni/Logaritmi | Difficulty: MEDIUM | Competency: logarithm definition understanding

**Q3** [Powers/Scientific notation] Il quadrato del numero 0,007 e...
- A: 4.9x10^-3 | B: 4.9x10^-4 | C: 4.9x10^-5 | D: 4.9x10^-6 | E: 4.9x10^-7
- **Correct: C** (0.007 = 7x10^-3, squared = 49x10^-6 = 4.9x10^-5)
- Topic: Numeri/Potenze | Difficulty: EASY-MEDIUM | Competency: mental arithmetic, scientific notation

**Q4** [Word problem/Fractions] Rita chocolate problem -- 4 equal piles for nephews + 1 half-pile for herself; then nephews arrive with friend, redistribute to 5 equal piles. Ratio of Rita's pile to one of the 5?
- A: 5/9 | B: 3/4 | C: 4/5 | D: 5/6 | E: 5/8
- **Correct: A** (Total = 4x + x/2 = 9x/2. Each of 5 = 4x/5. Rita's = x/2. Ratio = (x/2)/(4x/5) = 5/8... Actually: 4 piles of weight p each + Rita's pile = p/2. Total = 4p + p/2 = 9p/2. New 5 equal piles each = 4p/5. Ratio Rita/new = (p/2)/(4p/5) = 5/8)
- **Correct: E** (5/8)
- Topic: Numeri/Modellizzare | Difficulty: MEDIUM-HARD | Competency: word problem modeling, fraction arithmetic

**Q5** [Word problem/Arithmetic] Circolo ricreativo bus problem -- 120 people, buses carry 50 each, 600 EUR/day, 3-day trip. Cost per person?
- A: 45 | B: 15 | C: 30 | D: 36 | E: 48
- **Correct: A** (Need 3 buses for 120 people, 3 x 600 x 3 = 5400, 5400/120 = 45)
- Topic: Numeri/Modellizzare | Difficulty: EASY | Competency: multi-step arithmetic, rounding up

**Q6** [Algebra/Powers] Per ogni x, l'espressione (2x+2)^4 - (x+1)^4 e uguale a...
- A: 15(x+1)^4 | B: 7(x+1)^4 | C: (x+1)^4 | D: x^4+15 | E: 15x^4+15
- **Correct: A** (2x+2 = 2(x+1), so [2(x+1)]^4 - (x+1)^4 = 16(x+1)^4 - (x+1)^4 = 15(x+1)^4)
- Topic: Algebra/Espressioni | Difficulty: MEDIUM | Competency: factoring, recognizing structure

**Q7** [Inequalities] L'insieme delle soluzioni di una delle seguenti disequazioni e {x in R | 0 < x < 3}. Di quale?
- A: x^2-9x<0 | B: x^2-9<0 | C: x^2-3x<0 | D: x^2-3x>0 | E: x^2-9>0
- **Correct: C** (x^2-3x = x(x-3) < 0, solution: 0 < x < 3)
- Topic: Algebra/Disequazioni | Difficulty: MEDIUM | Competency: factoring quadratics, sign analysis

**Q8** [Quadratic equations] La minore delle soluzioni dell'equazione x^2 - x - 1 = 0 e...
- A: 1-sqrt(5) | B: (-1-sqrt(5))/2 | C: (-1+sqrt(5))/2 | D: (1-sqrt(5))/2 | E: -1-sqrt(5)
- **Correct: D** (x = (1 +/- sqrt(5))/2, smaller is (1-sqrt(5))/2)
- Topic: Algebra/Equazioni | Difficulty: EASY-MEDIUM | Competency: quadratic formula

**Q9** [Analytic geometry/Graph reading] Si consideri la retta in figura... (graph shows line with positive slope, positive y-intercept)
- A: y=3x+2 | B: y=-3x-2 | C: y=-2x+3 | D: y=2x-3 | E: y=2x+3
- **Correct: E** (from the figure: positive slope, positive y-intercept)
- Topic: Geometria/Analitica | Difficulty: EASY | Competency: graph reading, slope/intercept identification

**Q10** [Volume/Proportional reasoning] Recipiente cilindrico raggio 5cm con 1 litro. Quanta acqua in recipiente raggio 6cm per stessa altezza?
- A: 1.44 | B: 1.50 | C: 1.20 | D: 1.28 | E: 1.25

**Q11** [Geometry] Qual e l'area di un quadrato che ha i vertici su una circonferenza di raggio 4 cm?

**Q13** [Logic/Reasoning] "In ogni giorno d'estate c'e qualcuno che e infelice." Deduzione?

**Q15** [Functions + Analytic geometry] Graph of f(x)=1/x with line through two of its points at x=1/2 and x=3. Slope?

**Q16** [Exponential equations] Una sola delle seguenti equazioni ha una soluzione. Quale?

**Q17** [Statistics/Algebra word problem] Media voti = 25. Nuovo esame con 30, media sale a 26. Quanti esami in totale?

**Q18** [Geometry + Algebra] Cartoncino quadrato lato 1, taglio quadrato. Area rimasta = ?

**Q19** [Combinatorics] Quanti numeri dispari di quattro cifre con cifre 2,3,7,8 (each used once)?

## 3. QUESTION FORMAT AND STYLE

### Format Specifications
- **Type**: Multiple choice (scelta multipla)
- **Options**: Always 5 choices labeled A, B, C, D, E
- **Language**: Italian (mathematical notation is standard)
- **Calculator**: NOT allowed ("il cui uso non e consentito")
- **Figures**: Some questions include graphs, geometric figures, bar charts
- **Notation**: Standard mathematical notation (sqrt, log_b, fractions, etc.)

### Typical Question Stems (Italian wording patterns)
- "Quale delle seguenti affermazioni e corretta?" (Which statement is correct?)
- "E uguale a:" (Is equal to:)
- "Qual e l'area/la lunghezza/il rapporto..." (What is the area/length/ratio...)
- "Per ogni x, l'espressione ... e uguale a" (For every x, the expression equals)
- "L'insieme delle soluzioni di..." (The solution set of...)
- "La minore/maggiore delle soluzioni e..." (The smallest/largest solution is...)
- "Si consideri la retta in figura" (Consider the line in the figure)
- "Quanti numeri... si possono scrivere..." (How many numbers can be written...)
- "Da questa sola premessa possiamo dedurre che:" (From this premise alone we can deduce:)
- "Una sola delle seguenti equazioni ha una soluzione" (Only one has a solution)
- "Per una sola delle funzioni..." (For only one of the functions...)
- "Quanto manca al termine..." (How much time remains...)
- "In un'urna ci sono..." (In an urn there are...)

### Key Style Characteristics
1. **Mixed-topic questions**: A single question often combines concepts from multiple nuclei (e.g., Q15 combines functions + analytic geometry)
2. **Contextual problems**: Many questions are embedded in real-world contexts (chocolate distribution, bus trips, exam scores, driving tests)
3. **Distractor design**: Incorrect answers are designed around common mistakes (wrong sign, wrong exponent, forgetting to account for something)
4. **No calculus**: No derivatives, integrals, or limits -- strictly pre-calculus
5. **Mental math emphasis**: Designed so calculator is unnecessary; tests efficient calculation strategies

## 6. COMMON TRAPS AND PITFALLS

### Reported by students and preparation materials:

1. **Sign errors**: Forgetting negatives in quadratic formula, slope calculations
2. **Power/exponent confusion**: Mixing up (2x+2)^4 vs 2(x+1)^4 -- not recognizing 2x+2 = 2(x+1)
3. **Percentage traps**: Confusing "79% has passed" vs "79% remains" in time problems
4. **Rounding-up errors**: Bus problem -- not rounding up to whole buses (120/50 = 2.4 -> need 3 buses)
5. **Domain issues**: Forgetting sqrt(x) only defined for x>=0, log only for x>0
6. **Graph misreading**: Misidentifying slope sign, y-intercept vs x-intercept
7. **Logic pitfalls**: Confusing a statement with its converse or inverse (instead of contrapositive)
8. **Unit conversion errors**: Time problems mixing minutes/seconds, cm/m
9. **Counting overcounting**: In combinatorics, forgetting constraints (odd number => last digit must be odd)
10. **Calculator dependency**: Students accustomed to calculators freeze on mental arithmetic
11. **Random guessing penalty**: With -0.25 per wrong answer, random guessing (1/5 chance) yields expected value of 0, making it neutral, but students often guess worse than random when they eliminate options incorrectly
12. **Time management**: Spending too long on hard questions, leaving easy ones unanswered

## 7. TIME PRESSURE ASPECTS

### Structure
- **20 questions in 50 minutes** = 2 minutes 30 seconds per question
- Within the overall test: 50 questions in 110 minutes total (sections can be done in any order within their time block)

### Time Strategy Implications
- **Easy questions** (~25%): Should take 30-60 seconds each
- **Medium questions** (~50%): Should take 2-3 minutes each
- **Hard questions** (~25%): May take 3-5 minutes each; consider skipping
- **Optimal strategy**: Do easy ones first (build score safely), then medium, then attempt hard ones with remaining time
- **Skip threshold**: If after 30 seconds you have no idea, skip immediately (come back later)
- **Guessing threshold**: If you can eliminate 3 out of 5 options, it's worth guessing (50% chance = +0.5 expected vs -0.125 penalty)

### Key time advice from preparation sites:
- "Non perdere troppo tempo sui singoli quesiti" (Don't spend too much time on individual questions)
- "Se risultano particolarmente difficili andate avanti" (If particularly difficult, move on)
- Practice mental calculation speed specifically
- Learn to do rapid plausibility checks (estimation)

## 8. SCORING SYSTEM

| Event | Points |
|-------|--------|
| Correct answer | +1.00 |
| Wrong answer | -0.25 |
| No answer (blank) | 0.00 |

### Score Range
- **Maximum**: 20/20 (all correct)
- **Minimum**: -5/20 (all wrong)
- **Expected score from pure random guessing**: 0 (1/5 * 1 + 4/5 * -0.25 = 0)

### English section exception
- English section scored differently: +1 correct, 0 for wrong or blank (no penalty)

### What constitutes a good math score:
- **National average total (all sections)**: 17.9/50
- **OFA threshold (many universities)**: 7/20 in math section
- **Good competitive score**: ~14-16/20 in math
- **Math is typically the highest-scoring section** of TOLC-B (per Testbusters statistics)

## 10. COMPARISON WITH OTHER TOLC TESTS

### Structural Comparison

| Feature | TOLC-B | TOLC-I | TOLC-E | TOLC-S |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Math questions | 20 | 20 | 13 | 20 |
| Math time | 50 min | 50 min | 30 min | 50 min |
| Time/question | 2:30 | 2:30 | 2:18 | 2:30 |
| Section name | Matematica di Base | Matematica | Matematica | Matematica di Base |
| Total questions | 50 | 50 | 36 | 55 (new: Feb 2025) |
| Total time | 110 min | 110 min | 90 min | 120 min |
| Scoring | +1/0/-0.25 | +1/0/-0.25 | +1/0/-0.25 | +1/0/-0.25 |

### Math Content Comparison

| Topic | TOLC-B | TOLC-I | TOLC-E |
|-------|--------|--------|--------|
| Basic arithmetic | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Algebra (1st/2nd degree) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Plane geometry | Yes | Yes | Limited |
| Solid geometry | Yes | Yes | No |
| Analytic geometry (lines) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Analytic geometry (conics: ellipse, parabola) | Circles only | Yes (all conics) | Limited |
| Trigonometry | Basic (ratios only) | Full (formulas, equations) | No |
| Functions/Graphs | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Exponentials/Logarithms | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Probability | Basic | Basic | Yes |
| Statistics | Basic | Basic | Yes |
| Logic/Reasoning | In math section | Separate Logic section (10Q) | Separate Logic section (13Q) |
| Combinatorics advanced | No | Permutations/Combinations | Basic |

### Difficulty Calibration
- **TOLC-I math is HARDER**: Includes trigonometric formulas (addition, duplication, bisection), trigonometric equations/inequalities, all conic sections, permutations/combinations/variance
- **TOLC-B math is FOUNDATIONAL**: Described as "preparazione di base" -- first 3-4 years of secondary school; explicitly states it's "only a part of what's needed for Math/Physics degrees"
- **TOLC-E math is COMPARABLE in difficulty but FEWER questions**: Similar scope to TOLC-B for most topics, but with less geometry and no trigonometry; time pressure slightly higher (2:18 vs 2:30)
- **TOLC-S math is IDENTICAL to TOLC-B**: Same module, same questions, same database

### Average Scores (2024 data)
| Test | National Average (total) |
|------|------------------------|
| TOLC-I | 22.2/50 |
| TOLC-B | 17.9/50 |
| TOLC-E | 15.4/36 |
| TOLC-SU | 26.3/50 |

Note: TOLC-B has the lowest average per-question score among STEM tests, suggesting the biology/chemistry/physics sections drag down the average more than the math section does.
